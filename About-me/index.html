About Me

Dr. Pankaj Kumar Tiwari is an Assistant Professor at the Department of Basic Science and Humanities, Indian Institute of Information Technology, Bhagalpur. Originally from Siwan, Bihar, India, he holds a B.Sc. degree in Mathematics from Magadh University, Boadh Gaya. He received M.Sc. degree in Mathematics from the Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi. He received a Ph.D. degree in November, 2015 from the Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi. In his doctoral thesis, he studied water pollution and eutrophication in water bodies. The work included the possible control measures of water pollution and eutrophication. He received several scholarships and fellowships (UGC-JRF, GATE, NBHM and DS Kothari postdoc fellowships) from the Government of India and WWS2 post doc fellowship from the Italian Government. After completing his doctoral dissertation, he studied respiratory diseases and dengue disease transmission through mathematical and statistical modeling frameworks. He is actively working on mathematical modeling of ecological, epidemiological and eco-epidemiological problems.
